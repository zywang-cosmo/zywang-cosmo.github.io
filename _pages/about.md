I am a Postdoctoral Scholar Research Associate at the Infrared Processing and Analysis Center (IPAC), Caltech, working with Dr. Yun Wang. I received my Ph.D. in Astronomy and Astrophysics from the Pennsylvania State University in 2025, advised by Prof. Donghui Jeong, with a thesis on forward modeling large-scale structure in galaxy surveys with perturbation theory. Before that, I completed my B.S. in Physics at Tsinghua University and spent a summer as a research intern at MIT's Kavli Institute for Astrophysics and Space Research.

My research develops analytic and forward-modeling tools for extracting cosmological information from galaxy and supernova surveys. This spans cosmological perturbation theory and its extensions beyond the standard treatment, perturbative galaxy bias expansion and its renormalization, redshift-space distortions, and model-independent approaches to Type Ia supernova cosmology and dark energy. I am also interested in data denoising techniques for recovering information lost to nonlinear structure formation.

I work in Julia and Python, and I am always happy to talk about large-scale structure, perturbation theory, or anything cosmology.
