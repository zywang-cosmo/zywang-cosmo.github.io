Standard perturbation theory (SPT) breaks down on quasi-linear and mildly nonlinear scales, where its perturbative expansion diverges from N-body simulations well before the scales that are most informative for cosmological parameter inference. In this project I develop "perturbation theory remixed" (nEPT), a resummation scheme that reorganizes the SPT expansion to extend its regime of validity, first for the matter power spectrum {% cite wang2023ptremixed %} and then for the matter bispectrum {% cite wang2024ptremixedii %}. The approach captures nonlinear mode coupling more accurately than SPT at a given loop order, without the added computational cost of full N-body-calibrated emulators, and is validated against high-resolution simulations across a range of cosmologies and redshifts.
