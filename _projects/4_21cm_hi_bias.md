21cm intensity mapping surveys can lose the very largest-scale density modes to foreground contamination and instrumental effects, even though those modes carry unique cosmological information. With Donghui Jeong, I show that these missing large-scale modes can be statistically recovered from the small-scale "clustering fossils" they imprint on the short-wavelength density field through nonlinear mode coupling {% cite wang2024fossils %}. Separately, using N-body simulations and a semi-analytic galaxy formation model, I quantify the scale at which the linear bias approximation for neutral hydrogen (HI) breaks down, which sets a practical limit on the scales usable for cosmological analyses of HI intensity mapping data {% cite wang2021hibias %}.
