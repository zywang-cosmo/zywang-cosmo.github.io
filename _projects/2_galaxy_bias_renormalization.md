Galaxies and other tracers of large-scale structure are biased with respect to the underlying matter field, and the standard perturbative bias expansion introduces a hierarchy of bias parameters that are formally divergent and UV-sensitive at each order. In this project, with Donghui Jeong, Atsushi Taruya, Takahiro Nishimichi, and Ken Osato, I develop a renormalization scheme for the galaxy bias expansion that removes this UV sensitivity and yields bias parameters with a well-defined, simulation-independent meaning {% cite wang2026renormalized %}. This is a prerequisite for combining perturbation-theory predictions with realistic galaxy samples in upcoming spectroscopic and photometric surveys.
