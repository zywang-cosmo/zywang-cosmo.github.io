Most dark energy analyses fit Type Ia supernova distances to a parametric model (e.g. w0waCDM), which risks biasing the inferred expansion history when the true cosmology departs from the assumed functional form. With Yun Wang, I develop model-independent tools for supernova cosmology: a consistency-test framework, X(z), for comparing SNe Ia datasets and testing them against DESI BAO without assuming a dark energy parametrization {% cite wang2026modelindependent %}, and a lossless compression scheme that reduces SNe Ia distance measurements to a small set of parameters while retaining the full cosmological information content of the original data {% cite wang2026lossless %}. Together these let dark energy be constrained, and datasets cross-checked, without committing to a specific expansion-history model up front.
